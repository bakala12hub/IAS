Introduction to History

Historical Periods and Divisions

Bipartite Division

  • Pre-Historic Period: Time before written evidence (30,00,000 BC–600 BC).
  • Historic Period: Time with written evidence (600 BC–till date).

Tripartite Division

  • Pre-Historic Period: 30,00,000 BC–2,500 BC.
  • Proto-Historic Period: 2,500 BC–600 BC.
  • Historic Period: 600 BC–till date.

Chronology by Christoph Cellarius (1638–1707 AD)

  • Ancient India: Beginning–647 AD.
  • Medieval India: 647–1757.
  • Modern India: 1757–1990.
  • Post-Modern India: 1991–till date.

Phases of British Colonialism

  • Mercantile Phase: 1757–1813.
  • Industrial Phase: 1813–1858.
  • Financial Phase: 1860–1947.

Sources of Ancient Indian History

Archaeological Sources:

More reliable than literary sources. Examples: Coins, Inscriptions, Monuments.

Literary Sources:

  • Hindu Texts: Vedas, Brahmanas, Aranyakas, Upanishads.
  • Buddhist Texts: Tripitaka, Jataka Tales.
  • Jain Texts: Agamas.

National Movement Phases

  • Moderate Phase: 1885–1905.
  • Extremist Phase: 1905–1917.
  • Gandhian Era: 1917–1947.
