Introduction to Geography

Highlights of Geography

Origin of Geography

Term coined by Greek scholar Eratosthenes (276–194 B.C.). Derived from Greek words "Geo" (Earth) and "Graphos" (Description).

Contributions of Greek Scholars

  • Eratosthenes: Calculated Earth's circumference, called Father of Geography and Father of Geodesy.
  • Hecataeus: Wrote Ges Periodos (Description of Earth).
  • Hipparchus: Divided circles into 360°, invented Astrolabe for latitude and longitude.
  • Aristotle: Proved Earth’s spherical shape using reasoning.
  • Ptolemy: Famous Roman geographer and cartographer.
  • Strabo: Considered Earth oblong in shape.
  • Pliny: Wrote Historia Naturalia.

Indian Contributions in Geography

  • Aryabhatta (476 A.D.): Calculated Earth's circumference as 24,835 miles.
  • Varah Mihir (505–587 A.D.): Discussed eclipses in Panchasiddhantika.
  • Brahmagupta (598–665 A.D.): Wrote Brahma Siddhanta and Khand Kavya.
  • Bhaskaracharya (1114–1185 A.D.): Proposed Earth's spherical shape and gravitational force.

Arab Contributions to Geography

  • Al-Khwarizmi: Wrote Sind Hind.
  • Al-Baruni: Authored Kitab-al-Hind and Al-Qanun-al-Masudi.
  • Al-Balakhi: Prepared the first climatic atlas, Kitab-al-Ashkal.

Other Notable Contributions

  • Marco Polo: 13th-century traveler who explored from the Mediterranean to the Pacific.
